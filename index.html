F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions below

  • When are your office hours?

    Due to COVID-19 we are not planning to open our office in 2021 on grounds of customer safety. Alternatively, we will expect you to email us or drop off your tax information at our office door slide. This includes your W-2s, 1099's, 1099-Rs, 1095As (Obamacare Statements), Mortgage statements (1098s), student tuition statements (1098s), Student loan repayment statements, Interest and Dividend Income, Unemployment compensation statements (1099-G), and business income and loss statements. Once we receive your documents you will be contacted via email or by phone. Please allow us 72 hours to notify receipt of your documents. Click here for contact information.

  • What is the best phone number to reach you?

    Email is the preferred method of contact. Email clearly your questions including: first name, last name, and where you are calling from. If you think you cannot email me for any reason, call 240-744-2433 and leave a clear message. We will try to get back to you asap within 24 hours. However, we may not often be able to take live calls since the lines keep busy most of the time. In that case, leave a detailed message and it will be responded to.

  • I didn't get my stimulus payments last year, do I get them this time?

    If you were entitled to get it last year but didn't get it for any reason, you can claim the credit this year. However, if you were among those ineligable, you won't claim it this tax season as well. We will be updating you on this matter as the tax season progresses.

  • I was an Uber taxi driver last tax season and didn't do much this year to file my taxes. Am I still required to file my return?

    Yes. All you need to do is gather your Uber taxi fee income, no matter how small, and your unemployment compensation statement from your state government as well as any taxable items.

  • I didn't get my tax refunds for last year until now. What went wrong this year?

    Over a million people haven't received their refunds until recently. This was caused primarily due to the reportable health insurance coverage statement, 1095A. The 1095A, otherwise known as Obamacare, is a private health insurance coverage where the government contributes to the premium depending on the level of income. If this document were to be sent after the tax filing, it causes delay because it is processed manually. Be that as it may, those who expect the 1095A this year as well, you must bring it with all other tax documents for prompt processing.

  • When should we expect our refunds this tax season?

    We will provide updates on this matter as soon as possible. Those expecting earned income credit can expect to get your refunds in March per the IRS notices. So the sooner you apply the better! Earned income earners are often low-income families with children who often receive relatively larger refunds from the government upon filing their taxes.

  • How do I get my tax records once the return is done?

    Normally we will email your returns in a secured and password protected email. If you want to pick up a hard copy, we will make arrangements in order that you may come and pick it up. Else it will be mailed to you through the post office if you are far away from the area.

  • How much are your prices this year?

    Our prices are normally way below the market. We will update you on the market price on our website soon. We have added only 4 to 5 percent for inflation's sake from last year's charges!
